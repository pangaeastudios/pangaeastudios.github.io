Foreword


Among the hustle and bustle of your average city, an essential component to life is often disregarded and ignored. Under the shadows of skyscrapers that dot the concrete jungles of today; among the busy streets polluted with noise, light, and toxic gasses, the modern citizen is stuck in the daily cycles of metropolis. The modern man or woman is more connected to the world now than they have even been before, though nothing comes without a cost. The hyper-connectivity of technological society is marred by the loss of connection to our humble beginnings. For our home, the planet that gives us life, is bearing the consequences of our technical evolution. Our societies, often tied together through online social networks, face a constant struggle of discrimination, inequality, and systematic prejudice. These are symptoms of a society that has forgotten its humble beginnings. The beginnings of a species that now spans across all 7 continents, bearing 7.8 billion children, and inhabit all corners of the globe. What history teaches us is that all the land that we see, all the borders that we know, and all the seas that separate us, were all once part of the same initial whole; the story of humans holds no exception. We romanticize diversity, but fail to incorporate the very important aspect of unity. Pangaea aims to manifest the idea behind a popular Indonesian saying, a phrase that also acts as the country's constitutional foundation: "Bhinneka Tunggal Ika" ("Unity in Diversity). As we are all humans, diverse in our own special ways, but are united under the same form as beings of light and creation. From a single point, the universe expands, and into infinity we will disperse. We come from one, and we shall all eventually become part of one.


- We are Pangaea.
Why "PANGAEA"?


The name "Pangaea/Pangea" is derived from Ancient Greek 'pan' meaning "all, entire, or whole" and 'Gaia' meaning "Mother Earth or land". I chose this name for my creative studio because I wanted it to act as a reminder that although in modern times we see the world as a categorized landmass with separate continents, regions, and countries, that we are all ultimately children of Pangaea. The roots of life on Earth originated from this supercontinent and evolved with the planet to form the world that we know today. I found this idea to be very poetic, and also applicable to the nature of art in general.
